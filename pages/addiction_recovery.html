Understanding Addiction

Knowledge is the first step toward recovery

What is Addiction?

Addiction is a complex condition characterized by compulsive substance use despite harmful consequences. It's considered a brain disorder because it involves functional changes to brain circuits involved in reward, stress, and self-control.

Addiction is not a moral failing or lack of willpower - it's a chronic medical condition that requires comprehensive treatment and ongoing support.

Common Signs of Addiction

  • Inability to stop using despite wanting to
  • Spending excessive time obtaining/using substances
  • Neglecting responsibilities and relationships
  • Withdrawal symptoms when not using
  • Developing tolerance (needing more to achieve same effect)
  • Continuing use despite knowing the harm
